Choosing the right Sketchbook

Paper

Depending on the mediums you think you could use in this book you may choose a different weight of paper. I would recommend a medium weight mixed paper for most instances if you want it to work decently with any medium. An average sketchbook drawing paper can work well too. If needed you can glue in heavier weights of paper too.

Size

Up to what you think will work best for you and your partner. I would recommend to go with a medium size, too large and the piece might seem daunting or unachieveable, too small and you may feel confined.

Bindings

Choosing the right binding is a matter of balancing aesthetics and useability.

Spiral Bound

Won't have problems with paper getting curved from extra medium because the spine isn't locked in such a rigid order. Will lay flat when opened.

Hardbound

Much like a book, it has a solid cover over the spine. If adding lots of extra materials the size of the pages will outgrow the binding and will curve over from the spine. May be useful to take out some pages before starting to alleviate it if it is potentially an issue. Because of the rigidity of the spine it may not lay flat when opened.

Self Bound

You can gather pieces of paper, either same or different, or magazine pages and scraps and build your own sketchbook to work in. More of a time consuming process. Can get a variety of paper types to work from or get exactly the type of paper you want in the format you want. Can also be a good way to separate finished works.
